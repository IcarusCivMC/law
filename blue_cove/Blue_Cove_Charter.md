# The Blue Cove Charter, initialized Nov. 26 2025

This document serves as a temporary charter to aid the reviving, constructing
and organizing of Blue Cove.
This document is to be rewritten upon reaching the activity milestone
mentioned in IV.A.
Everything listed in this charter is in compliance with the Icarus constitution
and should be referenced whenever applicable.

## I – Residence and Activity
A. A Resident is defined as a player who owns, rents or bought a home in
the Blue Cove region. Residents may have dual residence with other
nations and cities.

B. A resident is active in Blue Cove if they have performed one of the following tasks in the last 3 months:
- Man / own a shop.
- Help with / complete a public project in a significant way.
- Construct a home or public building.
- Recruit a new player.
- Being a member of a board

C. When a player or group of players is suspected of abusing residence and
activity in the form of a loophole, the mayor may deny the approval of
residence.

## II – Mayoral elections and the purpose of the mayor
A. Any active resident may run for mayor in a period of 24 hours after the signing of this document. After this period, a ranked choice voting period of 24 hours will be held. Only active residents may vote in
this election.

B. A mayoral term lasts for 2 months. Any active resident may campaign for
the mayoral elections during the last week of the mayoral term, with a
ranked choice voting election being held on the final day.

C. The mayor will have the power to accept or deny residence requests, or
to expedite this process to one or more active residents of their choice.

D. The Mayor may set up a board of directors for the following topics:
- Construction
- Economy
- Recruitment
- Events

E. The mayor may step down from their position at any time, causing a new election process to begin.

## III – The board of directors
A. After the mayoral elections, the mayor chooses a resident to run each
board. This does not need to be voted on. The board is split up in up to 4
categories:
- Construction
- Economy
- Recruitment
- Events

The mayor may choose not to add one or more of the categories to their
term’s board. A board must always have at least 2 categories.

B. Board directors may recruit residents to their board. This does not need
to be approved by the mayor.

## IV – Activity Milestone
A. The activity milestone will be reached when Blue Cove has over 10 active
residents for the period of 30 days.
